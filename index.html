Blink(); is a hardware kit & class that teaches hardware development for the popular Arduino platform by incrementally expanding on the simplest hardware project: blinking an LED. The kit includes all required hardware for the class: Arduino board, battery pack, housing, buttons, LEDs, & passive components. The kit is designed so that students can build it on their own, but the class works best in groups.

The Blink(); class is a set of written and video lectures providing complete instructions for each project, along with a dedicated forum for students work together & troubleshoot problems. From the first project, loading your first sketch to an Arduino to blink an LED, to the final project, building a functional laser tag game, students will learn the fundamental knowledge & skills required to build hardware with Arduino.

FAQ

Do I have to buy the kit?
No. In fact, there will be a RadioShack component list available for all required parts. The kit allows us to plan the class for standard hardware configurations to better anticipate problems that students will run into. We also hope to drive down the cost of components through economy of scale, and provide custom cases for some of the projects.
How much experience should I have to take this class?
We only assume that you're comfortable with computers. Specifically, you should be able to download, install, and configure software; plug in external hardware; and read & follow technical instructions (if other people ask for your help fixing their gadgets, you're likely well-prepared for this class). Otherwise, we assume you know nothing about programming and electronics; prior knowledge of these skills will just mean you move through the class faster.
Do I get high school/college credit for this class?
No. This class isn't affiliated with any learning institution. Moreover, it's based on a different model of learning than traditional classes; it isn't a "10 week" or "one semester" class - it takes as long as it takes, there is no speed limit. Rather than trying to drive all students towards and performing assessments at standard milestones, this class strives to increase learning and skill for all students by providing them with opportunities for greater responsibility in each project as they demonstrate readiness for it. Different people will learn different things from this class - and that's OK.
Why this project and this class?
Arduino is a very popular development platform that we've used before for some fun projects, but we've noticed among our friends and peers that a common pattern is to buy one and then forget it on your desk for several months while you figure out what to do with it. This class started from the idea: what are some cool projects you can build with Arduino to show your friends?
